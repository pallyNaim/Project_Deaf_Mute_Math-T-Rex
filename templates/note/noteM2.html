Note Money

Counting and Making Change

Imagine you want to buy some snacks at a mamak stall. The snacks cost RM2.50, and you give the cashier a RM5 note. To find out how much change you get back, you subtract the cost from what you gave. RM5 (what you gave) - RM2.50 (cost) = RM2.50 change. So, you get RM2.50 back! In Malaysia, we use both Ringgit (notes) and sen (coins) for our money.
